### Organisational Unit

The CD Laboratory for Physics-Based Machine Learning in Industrial Applications is based at the Institute for Thermodynamics and Sustainable Propulsion Systems and aims to improve the applicability of machine learning (ML) to engineering problems. Specific examples include numerical simulations in the fields of structural mechanics and fluid dynamics, as well as predictive simulation and control. Although recent research activities have demonstrated the enormous potential of physics-based ML, significant research effort is required to develop stable and generally applicable methods. The CD Lab’s holistic research approach focuses on the possibilities of integrating physics-based information throughout the entire ML process, including problem formulation, data generation and preparation, ML architecture, and the training process. Through comprehensive basic research, novel ML approaches are being explored that incorporate not only physical information but also elements of numerical simulation to improve the training process. These research activities will significantly expand the extrapolation capabilities of ML models to enable reliable predictions in scenarios or under conditions that are underrepresented or absent in the underlying training data.
