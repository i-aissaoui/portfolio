# PhD Position: Emotion Understanding in Videos (LIRIS - Lyon 2)

## 📌 Job Summary
*   **Institution:** Université Lumière Lyon 2 / LIRIS Laboratory
*   **Project:** AIMAINT (ANR International Project)
*   **Location:** Bron (Lyon), France
*   **Supervisor:** Carlos Crispim (carlos.crispimjunior@univ-lyon2.fr)
*   **Focus:** Facial Expression Recognition (FER) for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

## 🔬 Scientific Objectives
1.  **Generic vs. Specialized FER:** Adapting models from exaggerated movie-style emotions to subtle, day-to-day medical interactions.
2.  **Domain Generalization:** Handling data outside the training distribution (different lighting, camera angles, occlusion).
3.  **Video Representation Learning:** Understanding temporal patterns and emotional synchrony in video streams.
4.  **Multimodal Foundation Models:** Adapting visual/multimodal models for emotion detection.

## 🛠 Required Skills
*   Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, Git, Linux.
*   Background in Computer Vision & Image Processing.
*   Ability to work in interdisciplinary teams (Health + AI).

## 🎯 Candidate Fit (Ismail AISSAOUI)
*   **Facial Recognition (Recognizini):** You have already built an end-to-end facial recognition app with real-time feedback loops.
*   **Vision Transformers (ViT):** Your implementation of patch-based attention is the modern standard for video representation.
*   **Sequential Modeling (Mamba-SSM/xLSTM):** Your thesis work on "long-term temporal dependencies" is perfectly applicable to "emotion synchrony" in video sequences.
*   **Domain Generalization:** Your work on the N-CMAPSS dataset (transferring between different turbine units) maps directly to the challenge of generalization across different clinical settings.
